Third Party Data¶

pynucastro incorporates the following publicly-available third-party data. Links to this data as well as citations to the relevant publications are as follows.

Nuclear reaction rates from JINA Reaclib¶

The reaction rate parameterizations in pynucastro/library were obtained from the JINA Reaclib database.

Cyburt et al., ApJS 189 (2010) 240

Tabulated weak nuclear reaction rates¶

The weak reaction rate tables 23Na-23Ne_electroncapture.dat and 23Ne-23Na_betadecay.dat were obtained from:

http://www.phys.chs.nihon-u.ac.jp/suzuki/data2/link.html

Suzuki et al., ApJ 817 (2016) 163

Physical constants from CODATA¶

The standalone Fortran networks and the StarKiller Microphysics networks rely on physical constants sourced from the 2014 CODATA recommended values.

P.J. Mohr, D.B. Newell, and B.N. Taylor (2016), “The 2014 CODATA Recommended Values of the Fundamental Physical Constants”

Mohr et al., Rev. Mod. Phys. 88, 035009

Nuclear binding energies from the Atomic Mass Data Center¶

The standalone Fortran networks and the StarKiller Microphysics networks rely on binding energy per nucleon sourced from the Atomic Mass Evaluation 2016 (default) and 2012 of the Atomic Mass Data Center.

The Atomic Mass Evaluation 2016 and 2012 are available online.

Atomic Mass Evaluation 2016:

  • Huang et al., Chinese Physics C 41, 030002

  • Wang et al., Chinese Physics C 41, 030003

Atomic Mass Evaluation 2012:

  • Audi et al., Chinese Physics C 36, 1287-1602

  • Wang et al., Chinese Physics C 36, 1603-2014
